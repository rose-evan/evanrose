Incentives and Trust

June 2026

The easiest systems to ship are often the hardest systems to trust. They can look complete while hiding the parts that matter: what the system optimizes for, where it fails, and who pays the cost when it does.

I keep coming back to incentives because they quietly shape the product before the interface does. A team that rewards speed above legibility will build fast things that are hard to reason about. A team that rewards careful implementation will ship more slowly at first, but the work compounds because people can understand it.

Trust is not polish. Trust is the result of being able to inspect the system, predict its behavior, and recover when the prediction is wrong.
